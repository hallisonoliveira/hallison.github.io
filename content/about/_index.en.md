I began my journey not in front of a screen, but over drafting tables. The transition from physical architecture to software engineering was driven by a fascination with systems that can evolve at the speed of thought. My approach to development is rooted in the principles of Brutalism: honesty in materials, clarity of function, and a rejection of unnecessary ornamentation.

At The Digital Atelier, I explore the intersection of high-performance systems and intentional digital minimalism. Whether I am fine-tuning a Rust backend or crafting an accessible UI, the goal is always the same: to create software that feels as permanent and considered as a well-built library.
